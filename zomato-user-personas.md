### Introduction:
These personas were developed based on my personal product observation of Zomato (Day 3) and the PAIR Guidebook's guidance on user-centered AI design.

### Persona 1: The Burned-Out Developer
**NAME:** Abhay
**AGE:** 25
**OCCUPATION:** SDE (Software Development Engineer)
**LOCATION:** Bengaluru
**TECH COMFORT:** High
**ABOUT THEM:** Abhay pulls 10-hour shifts and has zero energy left to cook when he gets home. He uses Zomato daily, but he experiences high decision fatigue the moment he opens the app and doesn't know what to pick.
**JOBS TO BE DONE:**
 * When I come home after a long shift, I want 3-4 highly relevant recommendations based on my budget, so I can order quickly before I get hangry.
 * When I’m ready to check out, I want to be sure I’m getting the best deal automatically, so I don't feel like I'm wasting money by missing a coupon.

**PAIN POINTS:** 
1. Endless scrolling just to find one perfect meal.
2. Having to manually hunt for coupons or accidentally missing out on discounts.
3. Too many ads, plus the dining-out discounts aren't as competitive as what competitors like Swiggy offer.

**WHAT THEY DO TODAY:** After scrolling until he’s frustrated, he usually just gives up and orders the same thing he got last time. If he’s highly annoyed, he’ll just close the app and make Maggi or walk to a nearby hotel.

**WHAT GOOD LOOKS LIKE:** An AI assistant or chatbot that suggests the 3-4 best meals based on his budget and automatically applies the best discounts. Even a simple pop-up saying "Hey, use this coupon!" would save him the friction of switching between apps to find a deal.

**MENTAL MODEL:** 
* **Expectation:** He expects the AI to understand his taste profile and find food that fits his specific cravings and constraints.

  ***Control:** He still wants to be the ultimate decision-maker on the final billing and have the power to swap a restaurant or a dish at the very last second.

### **Persona 2: The Overwhelmed Professor**
**NAME:** Arun
**AGE:** 40
**OCCUPATION:** College Professor
**LOCATION:** Bengaluru
**TECH COMFORT:** Low
**ABOUT THEM:** Arun is a college professor with a family of 4 who uses Zomato once every week or two. He usually orders on Sundays to relax and enjoy a peaceful day with his family. However, he finds the app's interface incredibly overwhelming due to the sheer volume of options and categories competing for his attention.
**JOBS TO BE DONE:** 
* When I want to order an afternoon meal or late-night dessert for my family, I want the app to intuitively recommend the best options, so I can order peacefully without getting confused by the tech.
* When I am ready to pay, I want discounts to be applied automatically, so I can save money without having to hunt for promo codes.

**PAIN POINTS:** 
1. A very cluttered homepage with too many flashing options and banners.
2. No AI or smart assistance to guide him through the ordering journey.
3. Constantly needing to ask someone else for help to finish an order.

**WHAT THEY DO TODAY:** He usually has to ask his kids for help to place the order. If they aren't around, he abandons the app and either cooks at home or takes the family out to eat at a nearby hotel.

**WHAT GOOD LOOKS LIKE:** A much simpler interface or a conversational AI assistant (via voice or text). If he could just state what he wants and the app did the rest—including auto-applying coupons—he could order independently without headaches or wasted money.

**MENTAL MODEL:** 
* **Expectation:** He expects the AI to communicate in simple, natural English or his local language, using either text or voice.

  *** Control:** He wants to stay in complete control of the final bill and the payment process.

### Key Insight:
Both of these user segments are deeply frustrated by Zomato's crowded home page. The AI needs to act like a helpful waiter—it should capture intent (by text or voice), curate just 3 or 4 highly relevant options, and automatically apply the best coupon to eliminate search friction.

**What is the ONE thing both Abhay and Arun need?**
A completely effortless shortcut to checkout. Even though Abhay is a fast-moving techie and Arun is a less-technical professor, they both just want the app to hand them a few perfect food choices with the discount already added, so they can simply review the final bill and pay.
